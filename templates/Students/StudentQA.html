  • question_answerWhat Is the Difference Between Warm-Up Challenges and Serious Challenges?
    The difference between a Warm-Up and a Serious Challenge is that a Warm-Up Challenge is intended to be used as a practicing and self-check tool. Warm-Up Challenges may be re-taken as often as needed, and often give credits from successful attempts.

    It is highly recommended to use Warm-Up Challenges to study for Serious Challenges or Exams in your course. A Serious Challenge is intended to grade your knowledge and skills on a topic for a grade.
  • question_answerWhat Are Parson's Problems and How Do I Solve Them?
    Parsons problems are problems in which you have to order code "code lines" in a correct order. You have to drag and drop the given "code blocks" onto the silver-grey box on the right. Please pay attention to indentation, as it may be graded.

    When you press the "Get Feedback" button, each wrong line will be highlighted in red to indicate that the line is wrong. If your order is considered correct the code will be highlighted in green. Note that each time you press the "Get Feedback" button, if the code is incorrect this will halve the points.
  • question_answerHow Is a Parson's Problem Graded?
    Each time you press the "Get Feedback" button, if the code is incorrect this will halve the points. If the problem was correct, then you will receive full credit.

    If “Code Indentation” is set to “true” by the instructor, indentation errors in the your solutions are counted against you at the rate of 1 point deduction per N lines of code divided by half, i.e. (1/N)*(1/2).

    Each "code line" or "code block" error is a deduction of (1/N). Each "code line" or "code block" under or over the required number of correct lines is also a deduction of (1/N).
  • question_answerWhat Is the Meaning of the Different Colors in My Progress Bar in the Achievement Page?
    The progress bar in the Achievements page consists of four parts displayed in different colors:
    • The course points earned so far (purple).
    • The amount of course points that can be earned in the future (yellow).
    • The amount of points already lost (red).
    • The learning predictor shows the total amount of points that would be earned in this course if you maintain your current performance (dark blue).
  • question_answerWhat is Dueling?
    Dueling is a gamification feature that allows students to challenge each other to obtain higher scores on assignments for Virtual Currency. Whether or not you want to duel other students is entirely up to you, with very little Instructor involvement in the process. The primary goal of Dueling is to provide students with an incentive and increase their competitiveness in a course.
  • question_answerAm I obligated to accept Duel requests?
    No. If a student declines or doesn’t respond to another student’s challenge, the duel is void and no Virtual Currency is awarded. To decline a challenge, go to the Challenge Classmates page and click the View button on the duel you wish to decline. Simply click the Decline button to decline the challenge and remove it from your Challenge Classmates page.
  • question_answerWhat can I Duel over?
    You can only duel over Warm-Up Challenges both you and your opponent have not completed. Also, you cannot select specific challenges; instead, challenges are selected at random by challenge Topic (ex. Addition, Multiplication). If either you or your opponent have completed a challenge, it is automatically ineligible to be available for the duel.
  • question_answerIf we’re anonymous, how will I know who I’m challenging to a Duel?
    There are two options: revealing your identity to your desired opponent, or maintaining anonymity and selecting an opponent at random. When selecting someone to challenge, the only information given is their Avatar. You would have to ask your opponent to reveal their Avatar to you in order to challenge specifically them. Otherwise, you have to select a random student to be your opponent.
  • question_answerHow do I start a Duel?
    In the Tools menu, click ChallengeClassmates to be taken to the Dueling page. To start a duel, click on the Create Challenge button and then select the challenge type Duel. You’ll be taken to the Create Duel page. If you have a specific student you wish to challenge, you must know their Avatar for the Select Chalengee option. The Select Topic option specifies which Warm-Up topic the challenge will be pulled from. The Bet VC checkbox enables the option for you to wager VC with your opponent. Note that you can only wager an amount if both you and your opponent have the amount set. Click Confirm to send the duel request to your opponent.
  • question_answerHow can I decline a Duel?
    In the Dueling page, click on Requested Duels to view all the duels sent to you. Click on the duel you wish to reject and simply click the Decline button. The Duel request will be removed from both your page and your would be opponent’s.
  • question_answerWhat is OneUp Chat?
    Oneup Chat is a online chatroom for OneUp that allows Instructors and Students of a course to communicate outside of the classroom. The primary use of the Chat is for students, like yourself, to ask the Instructor questions outside of class. Instructors can also create live discussion channels, have students post questions to different channels be answered during class, and create channels for different groups for group projects.
  • question_answerHow do I access Chat?
    The Chat is course specific, so first make sure you are within a course. Then, click Chat on the OneUp toolbar to be taken to the course Chat.
  • question_answerCan I create my own channel?
    Currently, only Instructors can create channels for students. You can ask your Instructor if you wish for a channel to be created. Your cool channel for cool students will have to wait.
  • question_answerCan other students tell who I am?
    No, students are anonymous to each other. Only the Instructor can see student names in all channels. The only way for students to distinguish the difference between each other is their Avatars. If desired, you could reveal which Avatar you have to other students and abandon your anonymity.
